
# Amanda Natividad — Audience Research Brief

## Core Idea

Being on LinkedIn does not automatically mean LinkedIn is the best marketing strategy.

The real question is:

> Where does your audience find ideas they trust enough to repeat internally?

---

## Main Insight

Example audience:

- Mid-market RevOps leaders
- B2B SaaS companies

Research showed:

- 80% use LinkedIn.
- Slack usage over-indexes by 201%.

This does not mean marketers should build a Slack strategy.

It means these buyers work in fast-moving environments where internal conversations influence decisions.

---

## Key Principle

People do not simply share content because it is clickable.

They share content because it is useful in internal conversations.

Your content should be:

- Forwardable.
- Discussable.
- Repeatable.

The goal is to create something someone sends to a coworker.

Examples:

- "This is exactly what I've been trying to explain."
- "Can we discuss this during our team meeting?"

---

## Forwardable Content Assets

Examples of content that travels inside organizations:

- Benchmark reports.
- Calculators.
- Diagnostics.
- Contrarian industry opinions.
- Mistake teardowns.
- Comparison guides.

These assets help buyers win internal discussions.

---

## B2B Marketing Lesson

Distribution channels matter.

But buying decisions often happen somewhere else:

- Slack.
- Team meetings.
- Internal documents.
- Executive conversations.

The winning content is not merely optimized for engagement.

It is optimized for internal sharing.

---

## Memorable Quotes

> "Where does this audience find ideas they trust enough to repeat?"

> "The asset you create should not just be clickable. It should be forwardable."

> "LinkedIn belongs in the plan. But it's not the whole strategy."

---

# Content Framework

1. Identify the audience.
2. Research their behavior.
3. Discover where decisions happen.
4. Create assets that help internal conversations.
5. Make content easy to share.
6. Become the source people bring into meetings.

---

# Practical Applications

### Content ideas:

- Industry benchmark report.
- ROI calculator.
- "Most companies get this wrong" teardown.
- Comparison framework.
- Decision-making checklist.

### Ask:

- Would someone send this to their boss?
- Would someone share this in Slack?
- Would this help someone win an internal argument?

If the answer is yes, the content has leverage.

---

# One-Sentence Takeaway

The best B2B content isn't designed to get clicks.

It's designed to get forwarded.
