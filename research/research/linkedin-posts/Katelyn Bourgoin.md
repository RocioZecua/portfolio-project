
# Katelyn Bourgoin — Repetition Is Strategic

## Core Idea

Repetition is not a sign of weak marketing.

It is one of the strongest marketing tools available.

The more often people hear the same idea, the more familiar it becomes.

And familiarity creates trust.

---

## The Principle

People rarely believe something the first time they hear it.

But repeated exposure makes ideas feel:

- Familiar.
- Credible.
- Memorable.

Over time:

> Familiarity becomes perceived truth.

This is why politicians repeat the same talking points repeatedly.

Not because they lack new ideas.

Because repetition works.

---

## Marketing Lesson

Many marketers constantly chase:

- New angles.
- New messaging.
- New positioning.
- New campaigns.

Meanwhile, their audience has barely heard the original message.

The market usually needs far more repetition than marketers are comfortable giving.

---

## Why Repetition Works

### 1. Builds familiarity

People trust what they recognize.

### 2. Increases recall

The message becomes easier to remember.

### 3. Creates association

Your brand becomes connected to a specific idea.

### 4. Reinforces positioning

Repeated messages become part of your identity.

---

## Examples

- Volvo → Safety.
- Nike → Just Do It.
- Apple → Simplicity.
- HubSpot → Inbound marketing.

These brands repeated the same ideas for years.

---

## B2B Application

Most B2B companies abandon a message too early.

They publish:

- One LinkedIn post.
- One webinar.
- One case study.

Then move on.

Instead:

- Repeat the core insight.
- Change the format.
- Keep the message.

One idea can become:

- LinkedIn posts.
- Podcasts.
- Videos.
- Case studies.
- Newsletters.
- Presentations.

---

## Memorable Quotes

> "Repetition isn't lazy. It's strategic."

> "The more familiar an idea becomes, the more true it feels."

> "The market gets tired of your message long after your audience hears it."

---

# Content Framework

1. Identify one core idea.
2. Repeat it consistently.
3. Use different formats.
4. Reinforce the same positioning.
5. Build mental availability.

---

# Practical Questions

Ask yourself:

- What idea do I want to own?
- Have my customers heard it 20 times yet?
- Am I changing the message too quickly?

Most companies have a consistency problem, not an originality problem.

---

# One-Sentence Takeaway

The goal is not to constantly invent new messages.

The goal is to make one important message impossible to forget.
