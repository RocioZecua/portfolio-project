
# Richard van der Blom — Authority Beats Attention

## Core Idea

Most LinkedIn advice is designed for creators.

Not for complex B2B sales.

If you sell:

- Enterprise software
- Consulting services
- High-ticket solutions
- Long sales cycles
- Multi-stakeholder deals

Then chasing engagement can actively hurt your results.

---

## The Main Problem

Typical LinkedIn advice focuses on:

- Viral posts.
- Engagement pods.
- Vanity metrics.
- Cold DMs.
- Follower growth.

These tactics may work for:

- Low-ticket products.
- Personal brands.
- Creator businesses.

But complex B2B buying works differently.

---

## The Three Principles

> Authority beats attention.

> Trust beats traffic.

> Relevance beats reach.

The goal is not maximum visibility.

The goal is credibility with the right buyers.

---

## Why Complex B2B Is Different

Enterprise buyers face:

- Risk.
- Large budgets.
- Multiple stakeholders.
- Internal approval processes.
- Long evaluation periods.

They do not buy from the loudest company.

They buy from the safest expert.

---

## The Four Things That Actually Work

### 1. Chase Relevance, Not Reach

100 ideal buyers matter more than:

- 100,000 random impressions.
- Viral posts outside your market.

A small audience of decision-makers is more valuable than a large audience of strangers.

---

### 2. Help the Buying Committee

Content should answer questions such as:

- Is this safe?
- What is the ROI?
- How difficult is implementation?
- What are the risks?

Talk less about motivation.

Talk more about decisions.

---

### 3. Engage Where Buyers Already Talk

Comments can outperform posts.

Thoughtful comments:

- Build visibility.
- Demonstrate expertise.
- Create context.
- Start relationships.

Attention without relevance creates little value.

---

### 4. Turn Your Profile Into a Positioning Page

When someone visits your profile, they should immediately understand:

- Who you help.
- What problems you solve.
- Why you are credible.
- What results you deliver.

Your profile is often your most important sales page.

---

## B2B Marketing Lesson

LinkedIn is not primarily an influencer platform.

For B2B sellers, it is a credibility platform.

Every interaction either:

- Builds authority.
- Damages authority.
- Or does nothing.

Consistency compounds.

Credibility compounds.

Trust compounds.

---

## Memorable Quotes

# Content Framework

1. Identify the right buyers.
2. Build authority with them.
3. Create trust through expertise.
4. Help buying committees make decisions.
5. Optimize for credibility.
6. Let authority compound over time.

---

# Practical Applications

Instead of asking:

- How do I go viral?
- How do I get more followers?
- How do I increase impressions?

Ask:

- Would my ideal buyer care?
- Does this reduce perceived risk?
- Does this build trust?
- Does this strengthen my positioning?

---

# One-Sentence Takeaway

In complex B2B sales, the goal is not to become the loudest voice.

It is to become the safest expert.
