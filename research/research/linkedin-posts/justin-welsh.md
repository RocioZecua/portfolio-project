## Post 1

Topic:
Building success through consistency on LinkedIn

Summary:
Justin shares that when he started using social media, he had no audience and no engagement for months. Despite that, he continued writing consistently until he eventually grew to over 1 million followers. He explains that growth is not linear and most people give up too early during the invisible phase.

Key idea:
Success comes from consistency during the invisible phase when nobody is watching.

Insight for B2B SaaS:
Early-stage content will not show immediate results, but consistency builds long-term visibility and trust.

Why this matters:
Most creators quit before reaching visibility. Consistency is the main growth lever in organic LinkedIn strategy.

## Post 2
Topic: Starting before conditions are perfect in business

Summary: Justin explains that most people delay starting a business because they wait for the “right moment”—a perfect idea, more money, or a stable market. However, these conditions never truly arrive. Instead, successful founders start early, even when things feel incomplete or uncertain. They launch, sell before everything is polished, and learn through real execution rather than planning. Over time, this creates momentum, clarity, and traction. His own business was built through consistent daily action rather than a perfect strategy, eventually scaling to $10.9M+ in revenue and 45,000+ customers without sales calls.

Key idea: You don’t gain confidence before starting—you gain it by starting.

Insight for B2B SaaS: In SaaS, waiting for product perfection or ideal timing slows growth. Early launching and continuous iteration based on real user feedback create faster product-market clarity and stronger traction than overplanning.

Why this matters: Most founders over-prepare instead of shipping early, losing valuable time where real learning and market validation could be happening.

**Topic:** Customer awareness levels in marketing

**Summary:**
Justin explains that most creators fail at marketing because they sell too early to people who don’t fully understand their problem yet. He breaks marketing into five levels of awareness: from people who don’t know they have a problem, to those ready to buy. Each stage requires a different approach, from creating awareness of the problem to using proof and urgency to close sales.

**Key idea:**
You can’t sell to everyone the same way—you have to match your message to their level of awareness.

**Insight for B2B SaaS:**
In SaaS, messaging must change across the funnel: educate unaware users, clarify the problem for problem-aware users, and use proof for product-ready buyers.

**Why this matters:**
Most companies fail because they try to sell too early instead of guiding prospects step by step through the awareness journey.
