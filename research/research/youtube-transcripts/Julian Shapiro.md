
# Julian Shapiro — Interview Notes

## 1. Long-form content works for B2B

**High affinity, low volume.**

* Fewer customers.
* Higher customer value.
* Trust matters.

Long-form guides work especially well for:

* B2B SaaS.
* Agencies.
* High-ticket products.
* Developer tools.

---

## 2. You don't need the best content on the internet

> "It only has to be the best for your target audience."

You are not writing for everyone.

You are writing for:

* Founders.
* Engineers.
* Growth marketers.
* Your ideal customers.

---

## 3. Content builds trust

Most people never read an entire guide.

Instead, they think:

> "If someone spent this much effort writing about this topic, they probably know what they're talking about."

Depth signals:

* Expertise.
* Credibility.
* Authority.

---

## 4. Audience quality matters more than audience size

Example:

* Gary Vaynerchuk → larger audience.
* Tim Ferriss → smaller but more valuable audience.

100 ideal customers can be worth more than 100,000 followers.

---

## 5. Treat content like a landing page

Julian A/B tests:

* Introductions.
* Opening paragraphs.
* Content length.
* Structure.

He measures:

* Scroll depth.
* Reading behavior.
* Engagement.

Content should be optimized just like product pages.

---

## 6. Hacker News lessons

* Never ask for upvotes.
* Resubmit after a few weeks.
* The title matters enormously.

Effective titles often highlight problems:

* "X is broken."
* "Why X fails."
* "The problem with X."

Negative framing often generates curiosity and discussion.

---

## 7. SEO and reader experience often conflict

Google tends to reward:

* Short answers.
* Quick information.
* Multiple pages.

Julian prefers:

* Deep content.
* Excellent reading experience.
* Strong design.

His priorities:

1. Brand.
2. Quality.
3. SEO.

---

## 8. People don't need to read the entire guide

Many visitors:

* Skim the content.
* Read a few sections.
* Notice the design.
* Notice the depth.

The size of the guide itself becomes a signal of expertise.

---

## 9. Build an audience before you build a product

If you want to sell something someday:

* Start writing now.
* Share knowledge.
* Build trust.

When the product finally exists, you already have:

* Distribution.
* Credibility.
* An audience.
* Trust.

---

# Julian Shapiro's Core Principles

* Build trust before selling.
* Optimize for quality, not volume.
* Treat content as a product.
* Build a valuable audience.
* Think long term.
* Create assets that compound for years.

> "People don't buy because they finished your guide. They buy because your guide convinced them you know what you're talking about."
