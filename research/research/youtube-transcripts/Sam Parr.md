
# Sam Parr

## Core Idea

Copywriting is not writing words.

It's understanding what people want and using language to influence behavior.

---

## Key Principles

### 1. Headlines are everything

> "You've spent 80 cents of your dollar on the headline."

Spend more time on the hook than the body.

Examples:

* Everyone laughed when I sat down at the piano.
* At 60 mph, the loudest noise comes from the electric clock.

---

### 2. Mind the Gap

Create an unanswered question.

The reader must continue reading to resolve the tension.

Examples:

* They laughed at me...
* You're reading this because I want you to.

---

### 3. The Slippery Slope

Every sentence should make the reader read the next one.

* Sentence 1 → sentence 2.
* Sentence 2 → sentence 3.
* Sentence 3 → sentence 4.

The goal is momentum.

---

### 4. Long Copy Wins

Long is not bad.

Boring is bad.

If the copy is interesting, people will keep reading.

---

### 5. Story > Features

People buy stories.

Structure:

1. Problem.
2. Struggle.
3. Guide.
4. Solution.
5. Transformation.

The customer is the hero.

The product is the guide.

---

### 6. Speak to a Specific Person

"Niches make riches."

Bad:

* For entrepreneurs.

Good:

* If you make over $3M per year.

Specific audiences create higher engagement.

---

### 7. Punch Them in the Face

The first sentence must grab attention.

Examples:

* Imagine storing your food in a toilet.
* You're reading this because I want you to.

---

### 8. Handle Objections Early

Answer:

* Why should I trust you?
* Why now?
* Why is it different?
* Why does it work?

Address objections before they ask.

---

### 9. Simple Writing Wins

Rules:

* 7th grade reading level.
* One idea per sentence.
* Short sentences.
* Fewer commas.
* More periods.

---

### 10. Rhythm Matters

Good writing sounds like music.

Short.

Medium sentence.

Longer sentence that builds anticipation.

Short again.

---

### 11. Cut Everything

Write freely.

Then remove 30%.

Then remove another 30%.

> "I'm not a great writer. I'm a great editor."

---

## Copywork

Sam's #1 recommendation.

Take great writing and copy it by hand.

Examples:

* Classic ads.
* Sales letters.
* SNL scripts.
* Great Gatsby.
* Newsletters.
* Stand-up comedy.

Do it for 30–60 minutes daily.
