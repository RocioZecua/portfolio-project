
# Shaan Puri — Storytelling Masterclass

## Core Ideas

### 1. Build a Binge Bank
Create content that compounds.

Even if individual posts perform poorly, they become assets. When someone discovers you, they can consume multiple pieces of content and quickly understand who you are.

**Goal:** After 1–2 hours of your content, people should think:
*"I really like this person."*

---

### 2. Intention + Obstacle

Every story needs:

- **Intention:** What does the character want?
- **Obstacle:** What stands in the way?

If either is unclear, attention disappears.

Examples:
- Harry Potter wants to survive.
- Voldemort wants to kill him.

Even small stories work:

- Want a croissant.
- Every bakery is closed.

---

### 3. Low Stakes Can Be Powerful

Stories do not need life-or-death situations.

Embarrassment, insecurity, rejection, or proving yourself are enough.

Relatable stories often outperform dramatic stories.

---

### 4. Write Like You Talk

Good writing:
- Simple.
- Conversational.
- Easy to read.

Technique:
1. Say the story out loud.
2. Record yourself if necessary.
3. Write down what you said.

---

### 5. Pacing Rule

> A story can only be as long as it remains interesting.

Length is not the problem.
Boredom is.

---

### 6. Hooks Are Overrated. Frames Matter More.

A hook grabs attention.

A frame makes the idea relevant.

Example:

❌ "Clubhouse's existential threat is time-to-value."

✅ "Everyone thinks Clubhouse will win. I think it will fail. Here's how it happens."

---

### 7. Get 100 Reps

Improve one thing every time.

- Better hook.
- Better pacing.
- Better ending.
- Better emotion.

This is the MrBeast approach:

> Make 100 pieces. Improve one thing each time.

---

### 8. Stakes = Emotion

Stories need consequences.

Not:
- Shark attacks.
- Avalanches.

But:
- Embarrassment.
- Failure.
- Rejection.
- Disappointment.
- Ego.

The audience must believe something matters.

---

### 9. Stories Are 5 Seconds of Change

A story is:

> A 5-second moment of transformation.

Examples:
- Scrooge hates Christmas → loves Christmas.
- The player becomes committed.
- The coward becomes brave.

Everything in the story leads to that moment.

---

### 10. Origin Stories Need Transformation

Structure:

1. Before.
2. Problem.
3. Lowest point.
4. Turning point.
5. Transformation.

People connect with:
- Struggle.
- Effort.
- Resistance.
- Growth.

---

### 11. Show Moments, Not Timelines

Don't say:

> "I wasn't doing well."

Say:

> "The new employee got promoted before me."

Specific moments create emotion.

---

### 12. Write for One Person

Not 100,000 readers.

Imagine:
- Jenny in her bedroom.
- Debbie at her desk.

One person.
One moment.
One emotion.

---

### 13. Start With Emotion

People share:

- LOL
- WTF
- OMG
- AWW

Ask:

> What should the reader feel?

Then write backward from that emotion.

---

### 14. Energy Matters

Content is energy transferred.

Before writing:
- Walk.
- Exercise.
- Dance.
- Change your state.

Writers often write tired.

Performers warm up.

Writers should too.

---

# Practical Framework

1. What does the character want?
2. What's stopping them?
3. What's at stake emotionally?
4. What is the 5-second moment of change?
5. What emotion should the audience feel?
6. Are you showing moments instead of summarizing?
7. Are you writing for one person?
